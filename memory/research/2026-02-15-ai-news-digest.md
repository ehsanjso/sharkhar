# AI News Digest - February 15, 2026

Quick scan of recent AI developments.

## Headlines

### NPR Host Sues Google Over AI Voice Clone
David Greene (former Morning Edition host) is suing Google claiming they illegally replicated his voice for NotebookLM's male podcast host. Google denies it, but Greene and colleagues say the resemblance is "uncanny."

**Implications:** More AI voice lawsuits coming. Voice actors and broadcasters increasingly protective of their vocal identity.

### Western Digital Sold Out Through 2026
AI data centers have gobbled up all enterprise HDD capacity for 2026. Consumer sales now just 5% of WD revenue. HDD prices up ~46% since September.

**Implications:** AI infrastructure buildout continues to strain hardware supply chains. Storage costs rising.

### Spotify: "Best Developers Haven't Written Code Since December"
CEO Gustav Söderström revealed top devs are 100% "vibe coding" - only generating and supervising AI code. No manual coding since December 2025.

**Implications:** "Vibe coding" becoming mainstream even at major tech companies. Developer role evolving toward AI supervision.

### Disney vs ByteDance's Seedance 2.0
Disney sent cease & desist to ByteDance over their new video generator making content with Spider-Man, Darth Vader, etc. Another AI IP infringement case.

### Gemini Experiencing Issues
Reports of outages and errors on Feb 14-15 (DownDetector spike). Unclear if widespread.
